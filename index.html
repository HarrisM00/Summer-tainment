Madrid, Spain

Main highlight of place: Bullfighting Museum of Madrid

Previous Next
Located in the Patio de Caballos, it offers its visitors an attractive tour through the history of bullfighting, demonstrating the relevance of Bullfighting in the history and culture of Spain. With free access, the eighth most visited monument in the Community of Madrid, in this space you can see engravings by Goya, dresses, capes and riding capes of great bullfighters of the 20th century or a corner dedicated to the figure of Manolete.
Here are some concise fun facts about bullfighting in Spain:

1. Bullfighting is a traditional Spanish spectacle dating back to ancient times.

2. It consists of three stages: lances, banderillas, and the matador's final act.

3. Matadors wear elaborate "suits of lights" (traje de luces) as part of their attire.

4. Bullfighting is controversial, with some regions banning or restricting it due to animal welfare concerns.

5. Despite the controversy, it remains an important cultural symbol in Spain, with colorful matador nicknames and passionate followers.
